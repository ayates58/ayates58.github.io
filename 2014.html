2014-15: Third Championship in Six Seasons

Regular Season Summary

The Blackhawks would finish the 2014-15 regular season with a record of 48-28-6. With this record, the Hawks qualified for the playoffs for six consecutive seasons. They finished in third place in the Central Division and earned a #4 seed in the Stanley Cup Playoffs.

Western Conference Quarterfinals

In the first round of the playoffs, #4 seed Chicago would face the #3 seed, Nashville Predators. These two teams met in the first round of the playoffs in 2010, which Chicago won in six games. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Chicago defeats Nashville 4-3 2OT (Chicago leads series 1-0)
  2. Nashville defeats Chicago 6-2 (Series tied 1-1)
  3. Chicago defeats Nashville 4-2 (Chicago leads series 2-1)
  4. Chicago defeats Nashville 3-2 3OT (Chicago leads series 3-1)
  5. Nashville defeats Chicago 5-2 (Chicago leads series 3-2)
  6. Chicago defeats Nashville 4-3 (Chicago wins series 4-2)

Chicago advances to the Western Conference Semifinals!

Western Conference Semifinals

In the Western Conference Semifinals, Chicago would be facing the #7 seed, Minnesota Wild. Minnesota defeated #2 seed St. Louis in six games to advance. This was the third year in a row that these two teams met in the playoffs, with the Blackhawks winning both previous series. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Chicago defeats Minnesota 4-3 (Chicago leads series 1-0)
  2. Chicago defeats Minnesota 4-1 (Chicago leads series 2-0)
  3. Chicago defeats Minnesota 1-0 (Chicago leads series 3-0)
  4. Chicago defeats Minnesota 4-3 (Chicago wins series 4-0)

Chicago advances to the Western Conference Finals!

Western Conference Finals

Chicago would taking on the #1 seed, Anaheim Ducks, in the Western Conference Finals. Anaheim defeated the Calgary Flames in five games to advance to the Conference Finals. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Anaheim defeats Chicago 4-1 (Anaheim leads series 1-0)
  2. Chicago defeats Anaheim 3-2 3OT (Series tied 1-1)
  3. Anaheim defeats Chicago 2-1 (Anaheim leads series 2-1)
  4. Chicago defeats Anaheim 5-4 2OT (Series tied 2-2)
  5. Anaheim defeats Chicago 5-4 OT (Anaheim leads series 3-2)
  6. Chicago defeats Anaheim 5-2 (Series tied 3-3)
  7. Chicago defeats Anaheim 5-3 (Chicago wins series 4-3)

Chicago wins their fourth Western Conference championship and advances to the Stanley Cup Finals!




In the 2015 Stanley Cup Finals, the Blackhawks would be taking on Eastern Conference champions, the Tampa Bay Lightning. Tampa Bay entered the playoffs as a #3 seed and eliminated the #1 seeded New York Rangers to make the finals. The Lightning would have home-ice advantage and the Finals were set to begin in Tampa.

In game one, Tampa Bay would have the lead 1-0 until late in the third period. Tuevo Teravainen and Antoine Vermette would each score a goal to put the Blackhawks up 2-1 and win the game to take the series lead.

In game two, the Lightning would rally back from a 2-0 deficit to win the game by a score of 4-3. The series tied 1-1 would now be heading to Chicago for games three and four.

In game three, both teams scored once in the first period and would remain tied until the third period. Brandon Saad would score 4:14 into the third period to give the Blackhawks the lead, but 13 seconds later, Tampa would tie the game. The game would remain tied until the Lightning scored at the 16:49 mark to take the lead. The Lightning would hang onto the lead and win the game 3-2. The Blackhawks would look to even the series at 2-2 with a win in game four.

In game four, The teams were once again tied at 1-1 heading into the third period. Brandon Saad would score a goal at the 6:22 mark in the third period to give Chicago the lead, and win. The series tied at 2-2 would now be heading to Tampa Bay for game five.

In game five, the teams were tied going into the third period until Antoine Vermette would score at 2:00 of the third period to give Chicago the lead. The Hawks would hold onto the lead and win the game 2-1 with a chance to win the series in game six.

Game six would be played in Chicago and give the Hawks a chance to win the Stanley Cup on home ice for the first time since 1938. This game would remain scoreless until the 17:13 mark of the second period when Duncan Keith scored a goal to give Chicago the lead 1-0. The next goal would come in the third period when Patrick Kane scored to increase Chicago’s lead to 2-0. This goal would crush any hopes Tampa Bay had of winning this game as Chicago was playing incredible defense and locked them down. Chicago would hold onto the lead and for the third time in six seasons, the Blackhawks won the Stanley Cup!
