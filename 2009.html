2009-10: Where the Dynasty Began

Regular Season Summary

After finishing the regular season with a record of 52-22-8, the Blackhawks would qualify for the playoffs in back-to-back years for the first time since 1996 and 1997. This team would set a franchise record for wins with 52 and win the Central Division for the first time since 1993. Not only did the Hawks qualify for the playoffs, but they would earn a #2 seed in the Western Conference.

Western Conference Quarterfinals

In the First Round of the Playoffs, #2 seed Chicago would face the #7 seed, Nashville Predators. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Nashville defeats Chicago 4-1 (Nashville leads series 1-0)
  2. Chicago defeats Nashville 2-0 (Series tied 1-1)
  3. Nashville defeats Chicago 4-1 (Nashville leads series 2-1)
  4. Chicago defeats Nashville 3-0 (Series tied 2-2)
  5. Chicago defeats Nashville 5-4 OT (Chicago leads series 3-2)
  6. Chicago defeats Nashville 5-3 (Chicago wins series 4-2)

Chicago advances to the Western Semifinals

Western Conference Semifinals

In the Western Semifinals, the Blackhawks would face the #3 seed, Vancouver Canucks. The Canucks earned a spot in the second round by defeating the Los Angeles Kings in six games. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Vancouver defeats Chicago 5-1 (Vancouver leads series 1-0)
  2. Chicago defeats Vancouver 4-2 (Series tied 1-1)
  3. Chicago defeats Vancouver 5-2 (Chicago leads series 2-1)
  4. Chicago defeats Vancouver 7-4 (Chicago leads series 3-1)
  5. Vancouver defeats Chicago 4-1 (Chicago leads series 3-2)
  6. Chicago defeats Vancouver 5-1 (Chicago wins series 4-2)

Chicago advances to the Western Conference Finals

Western Conference Finals

Chicago would face the #1 seed, San Jose Sharks in the Western Conference Finals. In the previous round, the Sharks defeated the Detroit Red Wings in five games. (Best-of-Seven Series)

  1. Chicago defeated San Jose 2-1 (Chicago leads series 1-0)
  2. Chicago defeated San Jose 4-2 (Chicago leads series 2-0)
  3. Chicago defeated San Jose 3-2 OT (Chicago leads series 3-0)
  4. Chicago defeated San Jose 4-2 (Chicago wins series 4-0)

For the first time in 17 years, Chicago wins the Western Conference and advances to the Stanley Cup Finals!



The Blackhawks would be facing the Eastern Conference champions, Philadelphia Flyers in the Stanley Cup Finals. The Flyers were considered a cinderella team as they were the last team to qualify for the playoffs. As a result, this earned them the #7 seed in the Eastern Conference. Chicago knew they could not take this team lightly and needed to jump ahead early. The Hawks did exactly that and won the first two games (6-5, 2-1) at home and would now head to Philadelphia for two games. The Flyers would answer in a big way and also win both of their home games 4-3 OT, and 5-3. The series would be tied at 2-2 heading back to Chicago for game five. In Game five, the Blackhawks took an early 3-0 lead and would continue piling on the goals. Philadelphia would respond with 2 goals, but by this time the lead was too big and Chicago won Game 5, 7-4.

Game six in Philadelphia was the last opportunity for the Flyers to win the series as they were down 3-2. Chicago scored a goal late in the first period, but Philadelphia would score 30 seconds before the end of the period. In the second period, the Flyers scored 8 minutes into the period only for the Blackhawks to respond with two goals of their own to take a lead of 3-2. This game would eventually head to overtime after the Flyers scored late in the 3rd period. Four minutes into the overtime period Patrick Kane got the puck, shot it, and ran off celebrating. No one could see where the puck was, but Patrick Kane knew he scored and replays confirmed this. Patrick Kane would score the game winning goal and give the Blackhawks their first Stanley Cup Championship since 1961!
